42%

of amika's customers first hear about the brand from their hairstylist. Word-of-mouth in the salon chair is amika's single biggest way of getting new customers — more than ads, more than social.

In December 2025, amika launched in 1,500+ Ulta Beauty stores — their biggest retail move in over a decade. But when a customer walks into Ulta, their stylist isn't there anymore.

what already exists
The amika pro app gives stylists education, community, and social media assets. The Ace chatbot helps shoppers find the right products on amika's website. The "On Your Wavelength" campaign shows real stylists recommending products at scale.
what doesn't exist yet
A way for a stylist to send a specific product recommendation to their individual client — something the client can take with them when they shop at Ulta, Sephora, or amika's website.
the opportunity
The stylist's recommendation is amika's most powerful marketing tool — but it disappears the moment the client walks out of the salon. The app and the chatbot are both there. The missing piece is the handoff between them — and that's a brand marketing problem to solve, not a technology one.

The problem in plain terms: amika invests a lot in training and supporting stylists. But the tools stylists have — social media assets, the pro app — are for reaching their own followers, not for passing a specific product recommendation to a specific client. When that client walks into Ulta, there's nothing from amika helping them find what their stylist told them to buy. That gap is the opportunity.

the opportunity

the bridge that
hasn't been built.

Three ideas that come directly from the insight — each one something the brand marketing team could actually test.

01
the stylist send
Give stylists a simple way to pass their recommendation to the client — a printed card with a QR code, a text template, or a shareable link from the pro app — that says "here's what I used on you today." The client can scan it at Ulta or shop from home. No new technology needed, just a new asset in the pro app resource section.
02
salon credibility at the shelf
At Ulta, amika sits next to 50 other brands with no sign of the stylist community that makes it special. Simple shelf cards, QR codes, or short videos linking to real stylist recommendations could bring that "salon-raised" story to life right in the aisle — for customers who never discovered amika through a salon.
03
"your stylist said so"
Every brand says "salon-quality." No brand can say "your specific stylist recommended this" — except amika. That's a message no competitor can copy, because none of them have a stylist community this deep. That's the campaign territory worth building toward.
